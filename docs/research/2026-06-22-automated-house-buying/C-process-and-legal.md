# NL Home-Buying: Full Process, Automation Surface & Legal/ToS Constraints

**Research date:** 2026-06-22
**Scope:** The full Dutch home-*buying* journey (owner-occupier, koop), how automatable each step is by an AI assistant, and the legal / terms-of-service / GDPR constraints a product would hit.
**Convention:** Facts are cited inline. Speculation / my own assessment is flagged `[ASSESSMENT]`. Figures are 2024-2026; market numbers move, so treat ranges as indicative.

---

## 1. The Full NL Buying Journey, Step by Step

The standard sequence for buying an existing home in NL. Total time from accepted offer to keys is typically **6-12 weeks** ([I amsterdam](https://www.iamsterdam.com/en/live-work-study/living/housing/steps-to-buying-a-house-in-the-netherlands); [Investropa](https://investropa.com/blogs/news/netherlands-long-buying-process-take)).

### Step 1 — Orientation & financing pre-check
Before searching seriously, buyers establish budget. A **mortgage advisor (hypotheekadviseur)** or bank estimates borrowing capacity; many buyers get a pre-approval-style indication so they can bid credibly. Mortgage advice itself is a **regulated activity** (see §3, Wft).
*Who:* buyer, mortgage advisor / bank.
*Cost:* advice fee is paid later at mortgage stage (€2,000-€4,500+ for expat files) ([Hanno](https://www.hanno.nl/en/expat-mortgages/costs-of-buying-a-house-in-the-netherlands/)).

### Step 2 — Finding listings (Funda + aggregators)
**Funda** is the dominant portal — ~200,000 listings, ~95% of all homes for sale, 30M+ visits/month, majority-owned by **NVM** (largest broker association) ([Imperva case study, 2019](https://www.imperva.com/resources/2020/01/Imperva_Funda_cs-.pdf)). Listings are placed by the **selling broker (verkoopmakelaar)**, not by private sellers directly in most cases.
*Who:* buyer (search), verkoopmakelaar (publishes).
*Cost:* free to browse.

### Step 3 — Viewing (bezichtiging)
Buyer requests a viewing. On Funda this is a **per-listing form** ("Plan een bezichtiging") that is emailed to the listing broker, who then phones/emails to schedule; if no button is present, the buyer contacts the broker directly ([Funda Help](https://help.funda.nl/s/article/360019248871-Hoe-kom-ik-via-de-app-in-contact-met-de-makelaar)). In hot markets viewings are tightly scheduled and competitive.
*Who:* buyer, verkoopmakelaar.
*Cost:* free.

### Step 4 — Making a bid (bod uitbrengen)
The bid is usually submitted **to the selling broker** by phone/email/form, and it is normally informal until accepted. A bid is not just a price — it includes **conditions (ontbindende voorwaarden)**: financing contingency, building-inspection contingency, desired transfer date, sometimes "sale of own home" contingency ([Notaris.nl](https://www.notaris.nl/wonen/koopcontract-en-overdracht/bedenktijd-bij-koop-woning)).

**Bidding format varies:**
- Most common: open negotiation via the broker (you don't see rivals' bids).
- **Sealed / "blind" bidding (inschrijving bij inschrijving):** all bidders submit one best offer by a deadline, not seeing each other — common in hot urban markets.
- Overbidding is normal: national average ~4.8-5% over asking in 2025; Utrecht ~13%, Amsterdam ~8%; two-thirds of homes sold over asking in Q1 2025 ([makelaaramsterdam](https://www.makelaaramsterdam.nl/en/slim-een-huis-kopen-hoeveel-overbieden-amsterdam/); [dutchreview](https://dutchreview.com/expat/housing/bidding-house-netherlands/)).

Many buyers hire an **aankoopmakelaar (buying agent)** to draft/strategize the bid.
*Who:* buyer (or their aankoopmakelaar), verkoopmakelaar.
*Cost:* aankoopmakelaar ~1%-2.5% of price or fixed €3,000-€7,000 if used ([Hanno](https://www.hanno.nl/en/expat-mortgages/costs-of-buying-a-house-in-the-netherlands/)).

### Step 5 — Offer accepted → Koopovereenkomst (purchase agreement)
Once price + conditions are agreed, a **written purchase agreement is mandatory** for a consumer home sale (verbal is not binding for residential) ([De Hypotheker](https://www.hypotheker.nl/begrippenlijst/hypotheek-afsluiten/voorlopig-koopcontract/)). It is almost always the **NVM/VBO/VastgoedPro standard model koopovereenkomst**, typically drafted by the selling broker. It states parties, property, price, conditions, transfer date, deposit/bank-guarantee clause (usually 10%), and penalty clause (usually 10% on default).
*Who:* both brokers, buyer, seller.
*Cost:* drafting normally included in broker services; no separate consumer fee.

### Step 6 — Bedenktijd (statutory 3-day cooling-off)
After the buyer receives a copy of the **signed** agreement, a **statutory cooling-off period of at least 3 days, including ≥2 working days**, applies. The buyer can withdraw **without reason and without penalty** during this window ([Notaris.nl](https://www.notaris.nl/wonen/bedenktijd-na-ondertekening-van-het-koopcontract); [Notaris.nl 2](https://www.notaris.nl/wonen/koopcontract-en-overdracht/bedenktijd-bij-koop-woning)). This is buyer-only and statutory (Art. 7:2 BW).
*Who:* buyer.
*Cost:* none.

### Step 7 — Bouwkundige keuring (structural survey)
Optional but strongly recommended (and sometimes a lender requirement). A building inspector checks foundation, roof, moisture, installations, etc. Often a **financing/inspection contingency** in the agreement lets the buyer back out if the report is bad. Sometimes done *before* bidding to price correctly.
*Who:* buyer, independent inspector.
*Cost:* ~€200-€500 depending on size ([dutchreview](https://dutchreview.com/expat/costs-when-buying-house-netherlands/); [HuisAssist](https://huisassist.nl/en/technical-inspection/costs/)).

### Step 8 — Financing / hypotheek
Buyer finalizes the mortgage with advisor/bank within the financing-contingency deadline. Requires a **valuation report (taxatierapport, ~€500-€750)**. Optional **NHG (Nationale Hypotheek Garantie)** for homes up to **€470,000 in 2026** (€498,200 with energy measures); premium 0.4% of the loan ([NHG](https://nhg.nl/nhg-actueel/nhg-grens-in-2026-vastgesteld-op-470000/); [Hanno](https://www.hanno.nl/en/expat-mortgages/costs-of-buying-a-house-in-the-netherlands/)).
*Who:* buyer, mortgage advisor, lender, valuer.
*Cost:* advice €2,000-€4,500; valuation ~€500-€750; NHG 0.4%.

### Step 9 — Notaris (transfer/akte van levering)
A Dutch **civil-law notary (notaris)** is **legally mandatory** to transfer real property. The notary prepares the **deed of transfer (akte van levering)** and the **mortgage deed (hypotheekakte)**, runs Kadaster (land registry) checks, handles the escrow of funds, and registers the transfer. The notary is impartial. Keys hand over at signing.
*Who:* notary (mandatory), buyer, seller, lender.
*Cost:* notary fees ~€1,000-€2,500 (transfer + mortgage deed) ([dutchreview costs](https://dutchreview.com/expat/costs-when-buying-house-netherlands/); [search summary](https://www.abnamro.nl/en/personal/mortgages/purchasing-costs.html)).

### Step 10 — Taxes & costs (kosten koper, k.k.)
"k.k." in a listing means buyer pays the transfer costs on top of price.
- **Overdrachtsbelasting (transfer tax): 2%** of price for an owner-occupied main residence.
- **Starter exemption:** buyers aged **18-35** pay **0% up to €555,000 in 2026** ([Investropa taxes](https://investropa.com/blogs/news/netherlands-property-taxes-fees); search summaries).
- Add notary, valuation, mortgage-advice, optional aankoopmakelaar, optional NHG.
**Total transaction costs typically ~5%-8% of price** for a main residence ([Hanno](https://www.hanno.nl/en/expat-mortgages/costs-of-buying-a-house-in-the-netherlands/)).

## 2. Automation Surface — per step

Rating scale: **Fully** (AI can do end-to-end with negligible legal/human dependency) · **Partially** (AI assists, human approval/professional needed at a gate) · **Human-required** (law or practical reality mandates a licensed human).

| Step | Automatable? | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Financing pre-check | **Partially** | AI can estimate borrowing capacity and gather docs, but *advice* on a specific mortgage product is regulated (Wft, §3). Generic estimate = fine; "you should take product X" = regulated. |
| 2. Finding listings | **Partially → Fully (with caveats)** | High-value, technically easy. BUT Funda blocks automated access (§3). Automating *search/monitoring/scoring* is the strongest value proposition — gated by ToS/anti-bot, not law. |
| 3. Viewing requests | **Partially** | Drafting + tracking deadlines is fully automatable. *Submitting* the request on the user's behalf via Funda's form/app is against the platform's anti-abuse posture (§3) and touches GDPR (acting as the user toward a third party). |
| 4. Bid drafting | **Partially** | AI can draft price + conditions, model overbid strategy, track sealed-bid deadlines. The *decision* to commit money and the legal weight of conditions should have human sign-off; an aankoopmakelaar's value-add is partly judgment + local intel. |
| 5. Koopovereenkomst | **Partially** | AI can *review/explain* the standard NVM model, flag missing contingencies, compare to norms. Drafting/negotiating the binding contract is normally the broker's role; the buyer signs personally. |
| 6. Bedenktijd | **Fully (tracking)** | Deadline tracking + reminders + a one-click withdrawal letter are trivially and safely automatable. The *decision* to withdraw is the user's. |
| 7. Structural survey | **Partially** | AI can find/book inspectors and parse the report into risks. The inspection itself is physical/human. |
| 8. Financing finalization | **Partially** | Document collection, deadline tracking, status chasing = automatable. The advice and lender underwriting = regulated/human. |
| 9. Notaris | **Human-required** | Property transfer **must** go through a civil-law notary by law. AI can prep/forward documents and explain the deeds, nothing more. |
| 10. Taxes & costs | **Fully (calculation)** | Computing k.k., transfer tax, starter exemption, NHG premium, total budget = deterministic and fully automatable. Filing/payment runs through the notary. |

**Where automation is genuinely high-value** `[ASSESSMENT]`: (a) **search + monitoring + scoring** of new listings against criteria; (b) **deadline / contingency / bedenktijd tracking** (a real failure mode for buyers); (c) **document handling** (collecting mortgage docs, parsing survey + koopovereenkomst); (d) **bid drafting + overbid modelling** as a draft for human approval. These are the parts professionals do least well at scale and where mistakes are costly.

**Where the law hard-stops automation:** the **notary** (mandatory human professional for transfer) and **regulated mortgage advice** (Wft licence). Everything else is "assist, human approves" territory, not legally blocked — the blockers there are **ToS + anti-bot + GDPR**, not statute.

## 3. Legal & ToS Constraints

### 3.1 Funda: scraping, automated access, the "unofficial API"
- **Funda actively blocks bots and has a litigation history.** Per the Imperva customer story, Funda layers bot management (Imperva, plus Akamai bot/captcha is visible in current page source) over its infra and has historically taken a **two-pronged approach: legal action + IP blocking** against scrapers. Funda explicitly worries about businesses "built around the relocation process" that **scrape lead data and then market to those prospects** — *exactly* the adjacency an automated buyer-assistant occupies ([Imperva case study](https://www.imperva.com/resources/2020/01/Imperva_Funda_cs-.pdf)). `[Important: this is the single most relevant source — Funda names this business model as a target.]`
- **Anti-bot is live today.** Requests with a normal user-agent return a captcha/verification interstitial ("We houden ons platform graag veilig en spamvrij … verifiëren dat onze bezoekers echte mensen zijn"), confirmed by fetching funda.nl pages and `robots.txt` itself, both of which served the Akamai-style human-verification page rather than content (observed 2026-06-22).
- **Database right (databankenrecht).** NVM holds the database right over current + historical listing data; Funda has an exclusive licence. Dutch courts have engaged with these rights (e.g. the VBO v. NVM/Funda competition case, Amsterdam Court of Appeal, 26 May 2020) ([Bird & Bird](https://www.twobirds.com/en/insights/2020/netherlands/amsterdam-court-of-appeal-rules-online-platform-funda-did-not-abuse-its-dominant-position); [Lexology](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=48c9d890-2364-44e9-969f-37c80089653d)). Systematic extraction of a "substantial part" of a protected database can infringe the sui generis database right (Databankenwet) — separate from, and on top of, ToS breach. Earlier Funda court wins reportedly limited scrapers to a single small image, 55 characters, and a backlink ([Imperva case study](https://www.imperva.com/resources/2020/01/Imperva_Funda_cs-.pdf)).
- **Unofficial mobile API.** Funda's app talks to backend endpoints; third parties have reverse-engineered these (and various commercial "Funda scraper APIs" exist — ScrapingBee, ZenRows, ProxyEmpire). **There is no official public Funda API for this.** Using the private app endpoints is unauthorized automated access → ToS breach + anti-bot evasion. `[ASSESSMENT]`
- **Is automated viewing-request submission against ToS?** `[ASSESSMENT]` Yes in substance. The viewing form is anti-spam-gated; programmatic submission is "spam"/automated-access the platform explicitly guards against, and it generates a lead a broker receives as if human. **Realistic risk:** IP/account blocking is near-certain at any volume; legal escalation is plausible given Funda's stated willingness to litigate, especially against a lead-touching business model. Low volume from a real user's own session is lower risk but still ToS-non-compliant.

### 3.2 GDPR / AVG implications
- **AVG = the Dutch implementation of GDPR**, supervised by the **Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens (AP)**, one of Europe's more active regulators ([Crowe Peak](https://www.crowe-peak.nl/en/knowledge-article/knowledge-article-tax-legal/privacy-en/gdpr-avg-ap-processing-and-data-breaches/); [Complico](https://complicoconsulting.com/netherlands-compare-privacy-laws/)).
- **You become a controller/processor** of the buyer's identity data (name, contact, income/financial data for mortgage, sometimes BSN). Financial data is sensitive-adjacent; BSN handling is tightly restricted under Dutch law. Need lawful basis (consent/contract), data minimisation, security, DPA, retention limits.
- **Automated contact on the user's behalf**: when the agent emails/forms a broker as the user, you process *both* the user's and the broker's personal data, and you represent the user to a third party. This is doable with consent + transparency, but the broker on the receiving end is also a data subject, and misrepresenting a bot as a human raises both ToS and arguably fairness/transparency issues. `[ASSESSMENT]`
- **Art. 22 (automated decisions):** a fully autonomous "bid/withdraw" decision with legal/financial effect on the user could engage Art. 22 safeguards; keeping a human in the loop sidesteps this. `[ASSESSMENT]`

### 3.3 Aankoopmakelaar / Wft financial-advice licensing
- **"Makelaar" is not a protected/licensed title** in NL the way notary is — anyone can broker; quality is signalled by NVM/VBO/VastgoedPro membership. So an **automated buying-assistant for search/viewing/bid drafting is not itself a licensed activity** `[ASSESSMENT]`.
- **Mortgage advice is hard-regulated.** Advising on or intermediating a mortgage requires an **AFM licence under the Wft (Wet op het financieel toezicht)**; conditions include competent advisers, professional indemnity insurance (BAV), controlled operations ([AFM](https://www.afm.nl/en/sector/themas/verplichtingen-voor-ondernemingen/melden-en-aanvragen/vergunningaanvraag); [Business.gov.nl](https://business.gov.nl/regulations/licence-financial-services-provider/)). A product that crosses from "estimate your budget / collect documents" into "here is the mortgage you should take" needs a licence or a licensed partner.

### 3.4 Anti-bot arms-race risk
Funda runs commercial bot management (Imperva historically; Akamai captcha visible now). DataDome/Akamai/Imperva-class defenses fingerprint TLS, headless browsers, behaviour, and distribute blocking. This is an **ongoing arms race**: evasion works until it doesn't, every block is also evidence Funda could use in litigation (the Imperva story explicitly frames bot data as litigation ammunition). `[ASSESSMENT: technically winnable short-term, strategically fragile and legally aggravating.]`

## 4. Risk Assessment (for a small operator) + Mitigations

Ranked by `[ASSESSMENT]` severity for a small operator selling this product.

1. **Funda ToS + database-right + litigation exposure (HIGH).** Funda names this exact business model as a target and has sued scrapers. *Mitigate:* don't scrape Funda as your data backbone. Options: (a) operate within the **user's own authenticated browser session** (BYO-session, user-initiated, low volume), making it "a tool the user runs" not "a scraping service"; (b) pursue **licensed data** (NVM/brainbay/Funda partner feeds) for the search side; (c) keep the product **assistive** (draft + notify) rather than autonomously submitting leads.

2. **Anti-bot blocking breaks the product (HIGH, operational).** Any server-side automated access to Funda will be blocked and degrade unpredictably. *Mitigate:* same as above — push automation client-side / user-session; avoid centralized scraping infra that presents one signature.

3. **Wft unlicensed-advice risk (MEDIUM-HIGH).** Drifting into mortgage advice without an AFM licence is a regulatory breach. *Mitigate:* hard boundary — "budget estimate + document logistics" only; **partner with a licensed mortgage advisor** for anything prescriptive; clear disclaimers.

4. **GDPR/AVG (MEDIUM).** Handling identity + financial data and contacting third parties on the user's behalf. *Mitigate:* explicit consent, data minimisation, no BSN unless strictly needed, DPA with any processors, transparency to brokers that an assistant is acting for a named buyer, **human-in-the-loop** for any legally-effective action (Art. 22).

5. **Liability for a bad autonomous action (MEDIUM).** An auto-submitted bid, a missed bedenktijd deadline, or wrong advice could cause real financial harm. *Mitigate:* **"assist, human approves" by design** — the user confirms every outbound bid/contact/withdrawal; the system tracks deadlines but the human commits. This single design choice neutralizes the Art. 22, liability, and "misrepresenting a bot" risks simultaneously.

**Design conclusion** `[ASSESSMENT]`: the defensible product is an **assistant, not an autonomous agent** — it shines at search/scoring (on licensed or user-session data), deadline/contingency tracking, document handling, and bid/letter *drafting*, with the human pressing send and a licensed advisor owning mortgage advice and the notary owning transfer. The "fully autonomous, scrapes Funda, auto-submits viewings and bids" version is where every serious legal, ToS, and regulatory risk concentrates at once.
